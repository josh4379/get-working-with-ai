The problem

Most AI rollouts stall in the evaluation phase

Six months of pilots, a dozen tools nobody agreed on, a bill nobody can explain, and a team still pasting work into a free chatbot. The blocker is almost never the technology. It's the absence of a decision, a budget guardrail, and a plan.

We turn “we should use AI” into a setup your team actually uses

One partner, one fixed price, one decision to make. We do the choosing, configuring, and training. You get a governed setup your finance team and your staff can both live with.

It starts with a one-to-one call on Teams or Zoom. We map what your people actually do all day, where AI genuinely helps and, just as importantly, where it doesn't. Then we recommend a provider, pick models that fit the work, and set hard spending limits before a single licence is bought.

Within 30 days you're live: admin portal configured, settings tuned, optional SSO so nobody manages another password, an implementation plan written for your organisation, and a plain-English guide that shows employees how to get real work done without burning budget.

FAQ

The AI implementation questions we get asked most

Straight answers on scope, timeline, cost, and what happens if it doesn't suit you. Anything else, email us before you buy.

What is included in the Clarity Package?

The business case and the blueprint. A 60-minute 1:1 call on Teams or Zoom, then two documents. The first is a written business case for AI in your company: the hours saved, the work that improves, and an honest list of what AI won't fix. The second is a detailed implementation document setting out the exact provider, the models matched to each job, the admin and security settings, the spending caps, and what your finished setup would look like. It's yours to keep, whether you build it in-house or hand it back to us. See a worked example of the business case and the implementation plan.

What is included in the Implementation Package?

The whole build, done for you. Provider chosen, bought and configured, models matched to jobs, hard spending limits set at the provider, admin portal done including roles, permissions, retention and optional SSO, your IT team worked with directly where you have one, and your staff enabled with a how-to guide built on their real tasks plus a live all-hands call. Hands-off for you, live in 30 days or less.

How long does AI implementation take?

Thirty days from kickoff to live, at the outside, and often less. The deadline is the point: it's what stops an AI project turning into an eighteen-month evaluation that quietly gets shelved.

Do we need AI expertise in-house?

None. That's the entire premise. We handle provider selection, model choices, admin configuration, spending controls, and staff enablement, and we hand back a setup your existing IT team can run without us.

How do you stop the AI bill running away?

Two ways. We match the right model to each job, since most overspending is a premium model doing trivial work, and we set hard limits at the provider so there's a ceiling, not just a dashboard you'd have to remember to check.

Which package should we start with?

Clarity if the decision isn't made or the budget isn't approved, since it hands you the business case and the full plan to take to whoever signs it off. Implementation if you're ready to go. Buying Clarity first and Implementation later is a normal path, and the discovery work carries over rather than being repeated.

What does it cost?

The Clarity Package is $500 USD and the Implementation Package is $5,000 USD. Both are one-off and fixed, with no hourly rates and no variable scope. Provider licence fees are paid directly to your provider and sit outside our fee; we size those with you so there are no surprises on either side.

We already have some AI licences. Is it too late?

No. It's the most common place we start. Scattered licences, no admin controls, and spend nobody can account for is a normal starting point. We audit what you already have, keep what's working, and fold the rest into one governed setup.
